Kingsport Amateur Radio Club – W4TRC
Kingsport, Tennessee – Established 1947

The Kingsport Amateur Radio Club (KARC), callsign W4TRC, serves amateur radio operators in Northeast Tennessee and Southwest Virginia. Founded in 1947, our club has a long tradition of promoting the art, science, and service of amateur radio while supporting our community through public service and emergency communications.

We maintain several repeaters on top of Bays Mountain serving the Tri-Cities area, including:

  • 146.970 MHz (-) PL 123 Hz

  • 443.325 MHz (+) PL 123 Hz

Our members are active in:

  • Public service and emergency communications

  • Skywarn severe weather reporting

  • ARRL Field Day and other special events

  • Community outreach and education

  • Licensing classes and VE testing sessions

Regular meetings are held on the second Thursday at 6:00 PM (dinner)/7:00 PM (meeting) at The Press Room at Food City, 300 Clinchfield, Kingsport, TN 37660. Visitors are always welcome — whether you are a licensed ham or simply interested in learning more about the hobby.

For more information, visit www.w4trc.org.
