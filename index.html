Kingsport Amateur Radio Club

Serving the greater Kingsport, Tennessee area since 1947 • Callsign: W4TRC

The Kingsport Amateur Radio Club (KARC) is a community of radio enthusiasts dedicated to learning, service, and on-air fun. From public-service communications and emergency readiness to contesting, portable ops, and mentoring, we welcome hams of all experience levels-- and anyone curious about getting licensed.

Weekly Nets

Sunday Evening Net -- 8:30 p.m. local on the W4TRC 146.970 MHz repeater (−600 kHz, 123.0 Hz tone). Announcements include club news and activities, test sessions, swap info, and more. First Sundays often include a 2-meter simplex net afterward (frequency announced on-air).

Club Meetings

We meet regularly in Kingsport, TN. Prospective members and visitors are encouraged to attend. For the current schedule and location, visit w4trc.org.

Repeaters

  • 146.970 MHz (−0.600, 123.0 Hz) -- Primary club repeater • Sunday net

Licensing, Mentoring & Education

Interested in earning your license or upgrading? Our members can help with study resources, on-air practice, and equipment advice. We announce local test sessions during our nets and on the club website.

Check w4trc.org for upcoming sessions and learning opportunities.

Join Us

Whether you’re brand new or Extra-class, there’s a place for you at KARC. Come to a meeting, check in to a net, or stop by a portable event to say hello.
